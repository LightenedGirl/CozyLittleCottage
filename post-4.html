5 Genius Water Storage Solutions for Apartments

April 28, 2026

*This post contains affiliate links. If you purchase through them, we may earn a small commission at no extra cost to you.*


Growing up, my family lived in a small apartment. Every now and then the local authorities would send out a notice warning residents to store enough water for a few days because of scheduled maintenance work on the water supply. The funny thing is, the water never actually got delayed for more than 24 hours. But that did not stop my parents from filling up every barrel, bucket, and container they could find — enough to last us a full week without a single drop from the tap.

 At the time I thought they were being overly cautious. Looking back, they were absolutely right. Water is the one thing you cannot improvise when it runs out. And the peace of mind that comes from knowing you have enough stored — even if you never end up needing it — is genuinely priceless.

 The challenge back then was space. We were in a small apartment with no garage and no basement, just like millions of households today. But my parents made it work. And in this post I want to show you exactly how you can too — with five practical water storage solutions designed specifically for small spaces, no matter how little room you have.

 I will cover exactly how much you need to store, what containers work best, where to put them in your apartment, and how to keep your supply safe and fresh over time.

How much water does one person actually need?

Before you buy a single container, you need to know your target. The standard recommendation from emergency agencies is one gallon(3-4 litres) of water per person per day. That covers drinking, cooking, and basic hygiene.

 For a single person, a two-week emergency supply is just 14 gallons. For a couple, 28 gallons. That sounds like a lot, but as you will see below, it fits very comfortably on a single closet shelf or under a bed.

  Do not forget pets. A medium-sized dog needs about half a gallon per day. Add that to your total before you start shopping.

Where exactly to store it in your apartment


Finding the right spots is half the battle. Here are the best locations in a typical apartment ranked by convenience:

1. Closet floor — the single best location. Cool, dark, and out of the way. Stack rigid jugs here.
2. Under the bed — use flat water bricks. Slides in and out without moving furniture.
3. Behind the sofa — a row of jugs fits neatly in the gap between the sofa and the wall.
4. Inside a storage ottoman — many ottomans hold two or three 3.5-gallon water bricks perfectly.
5. Pantry bottom shelf — keep one or two containers here for easy daily rotation.
6. Under the bathroom sink — store your WaterBOB bag here so it is always ready.

Avoid storing water near chemicals, cleaning products, petrol, or paint. Even sealed containers can absorb odours and compounds over time.

How to keep your stored water safe to drink

Tap water in most cities is already treated with chlorine, which gives it a reasonable shelf life in sealed containers. Here is everything you need to know:

  1. Label every container with the fill date before you seal it.
  2. Rotate your supply every 6 to 12 months. Use the old water for plants or cleaning, refill with fresh tap water.
  3. Store in a cool dark place away from sunlight, which degrades plastic and encourages algae growth.
  4. If you want extra peace of mind, add 8 drops of plain unscented liquid bleach (6% sodium hypochlorite) per gallon before sealing. This extends shelf life significantly
  5. If your stored water looks cloudy or smells off when you open it, filter it before drinking.
